<p><p>Waterborne polyurethane resin is used in the production of synthetic leather through two main processes: dry process and wet process. In the dry process, the resin is applied directly to a substrate and cured using heat, while in the wet process, the resin is dispersed in water and then coated onto the substrate before undergoing a drying and curing process. Both processes offer unique benefits and are commonly used in the synthetic leather industry to create durable and high-quality materials.</p></p>
