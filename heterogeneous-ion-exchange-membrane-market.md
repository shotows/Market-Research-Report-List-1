<p><p>Heterogeneous ion-exchange membrane market consists of two main types: unipolar membrane and bipolar membrane. Unipolar membranes are used to separate ions of the same charge, while bipolar membranes are designed to split water molecules into hydrogen and hydroxide ions. Unipolar membranes find applications in electrochemical devices like fuel cells, while bipolar membranes are used in processes like water electrolysis and desalination. Both types of membranes play a crucial role in various industries by facilitating selective ion transport and separation processes.</p></p>
