Thirdly, the rising prevalence of chronic diseases and age-related conditions, coupled with the growing geriatric population, is driving the demand for less invasive surgical techniques.</p><p>Moreover, the COVID-19 pandemic has further accelerated the adoption of minimally invasive surgical systems. Minimally invasive procedures proved to be a viable solution as they offered shorter hospital stays, fewer postoperative complications, and reduced the risk of infection.</p><p>A key trend in the Minimally Invasive Surgical Systems Market is the integration of robotic-assisted systems. <p><p>The Minimally Invasive Surgical Systems market comprises two major types: Surgical Robotic Systems and Conventional Minimally Invasive Surgical Systems. Surgical Robotic Systems involve the use of advanced robotics technology to perform surgery with precision and flexibility. These systems are controlled by surgeons and offer enhanced visualization and dexterity. On the other hand, Conventional Minimally Invasive Surgical Systems involve traditional surgical instruments and techniques, such as laparoscopy. These systems require larger incisions and rely on manual manipulation by surgeons. Both types aim to minimize trauma, scarring, and recovery time for patients.</p></p>
