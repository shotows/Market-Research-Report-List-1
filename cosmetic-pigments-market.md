<p><p>Cosmetic pigments are classified into two main categories: inorganic and organic. Inorganic pigments are derived from natural minerals and are known for their stability and durability. They are commonly used in products such as foundations, eyeliners, and lipsticks. Organic pigments, on the other hand, are synthetic compounds that provide brighter and more vibrant colors. They are often used in products like nail polishes, lip glosses, and eyeshadows. Both types of pigments play a crucial role in the cosmetic industry, offering a wide range of colors and effects for various makeup products.</p></p>
