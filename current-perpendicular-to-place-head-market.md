<p><p>A Current-Perpendicular-To-Place (CPP) head is a type of magnetic recording head used in hard disk drives to write data on a spinning disk. This technology allows for higher storage density and faster data transfer rates. The CPP head works by aligning the magnetic field perpendicular to the surface of the disk, which helps to reduce interference between adjacent tracks and improve overall data reliability.</p><p>The CPP head market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.5% during the forecast period.
