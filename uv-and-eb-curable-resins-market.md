<p><p>UV and EB curable resins are two types of resins used in various industries for coatings, adhesives, and printing inks. Radical curable resins are cured through a free radical polymerization process initiated by UV or EB radiation, while cationic curable resins undergo a cationic polymerization reaction. Radical curable resins typically offer fast curing times and good adhesion properties, while cationic curable resins are known for their excellent chemical resistance and low shrinkage during curing. Both types of resins are popular choices for applications that require quick and efficient curing processes.</p></p>
