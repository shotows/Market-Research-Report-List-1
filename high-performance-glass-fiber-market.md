<p><p>The high-performance glass fiber market can be classified into two types based on their composition: polymer composites and non-polymer composites. Polymer composites refer to glass fibers that are combined with a polymer matrix, resulting in a lightweight and durable material suitable for various applications such as automotive and aerospace industries. On the other hand, non-polymer composites involve the use of glass fibers combined with non-polymer matrices like metals or ceramics, offering superior strength, stiffness, and resistance to high temperatures, commonly used in industries such as construction and electrical engineering.</p></p>
