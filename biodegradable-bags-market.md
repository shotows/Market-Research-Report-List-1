<p><p>Biodegradable bags are becoming increasingly popular in the market as a sustainable alternative to traditional plastic bags. There are several types of biodegradable bags available. Poly Lactic Acid (PLA) bags are made from renewable resources such as cornstarch and sugarcane. Starch-based bags are derived from natural starches and are non-toxic. Polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA) bags are made from bacteria that produce a biopolymer. Polybutylene succinate (PBS) bags are petroleum-based but degrade in composting conditions. Cellulose-based bags are derived from plant cellulose and are compostable. Other types of biodegradable bags utilize different materials and manufacturing processes.</p></p>
