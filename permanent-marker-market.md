<p><p>Permanent markers come in two main types: liquid and solid. Liquid permanent markers are typically used for writing on surfaces such as paper, plastic, metal, and glass. They contain ink that is quick-drying and resistant to fading or smudging. On the other hand, solid permanent markers are commonly used for writing on rough surfaces like wood or stone. They contain a solid, opaque pigment that is waterproof and long-lasting. Both types of permanent markers have their own unique uses and advantages in the market.</p></p>
