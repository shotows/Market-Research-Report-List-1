<p><p>Solid lubricants are substances that reduce friction between moving surfaces without the need for liquid or oil-based lubrication. MoS2 (Molybdenum Disulfide) is a popular solid lubricant known for its unique lamellar structure, excellent high-temperature resistance, and low friction coefficient. PTFE (Polytetrafluoroethylene) is another widely used solid lubricant recognized for its non-stick properties and chemical inertness. Graphite is known for its self-lubricating properties and ability to withstand extreme temperatures. Soft metals, such as silver and lead, are also used as solid lubricants due to their low melting points and high ductility. Other solid lubricants include boron nitride, calcium fluoride, and tungsten disulfide.</p></p>
