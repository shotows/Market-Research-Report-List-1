<p><p>Seaweed hydrocolloids such as agar-agar, alginates, and carrageenan are popular types of additives used in the food and pharmaceutical industries. Agar-agar is a gelatinous substance derived from red algae, while alginates are extracted from brown algae and are known for their thickening and stabilizing properties. Carrageenan, another type of seaweed hydrocolloid, is commonly used as a thickening agent and vegan alternative to gelatin. These ingredients are valued for their versatility, functionality, and natural origin in various applications.</p></p>
