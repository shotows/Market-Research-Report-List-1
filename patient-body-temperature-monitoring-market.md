<p><p>The non-invasive temperature monitoring market involves monitoring a patient's body temperature without penetrating the skin. This can be done through methods such as forehead thermometers, infrared thermometers, or wearable temperature patches. Non-invasive temperature monitoring is preferred for its convenience and ease of use. In contrast, invasive temperature monitoring involves inserting a temperature probe into the patient's body, typically through the rectum, esophagus, or urinary tract. It provides more accurate temperature readings but is more invasive and requires skilled healthcare professionals to perform.</p></p>
