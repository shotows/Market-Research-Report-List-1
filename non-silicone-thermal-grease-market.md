<p><p>Non-silicone thermal grease is a type of thermal interface material used to efficiently transfer heat from electronic components to heat sinks. It is primarily made of zinc oxide or metal oxide fillers in a non-silicone base. Non-silicone thermal grease offers advantages such as higher thermal conductivity, stability at high temperatures, and corrosion resistance compared to silicone-based options.</p><p>The non-silicone thermal grease market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.5% during the forecast period. The increasing demand for electronic devices across various industries such as automotive, consumer electronics, and telecommunications is driving the growth of the market. Additionally, the growing trend of miniaturization of electronic components, which leads to higher thermal challenges, is further fueling the demand for non-silicone thermal grease.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the non-silicone thermal grease market is the development of advanced formulations with enhanced thermal conductivity and improved reliability.
