<p><p>Microbial Fuel Cells (MFCs) are devices that use bacteria to convert organic matter into electricity. There are two main types of MFCs based on the presence of mediators: Mediator Microbial Fuel Cell and Mediator-free Microbial Fuel Cell. </p><p>In Mediator MFCs, external mediators are used to facilitate electron transfer between the bacteria and the electrode, while in Mediator-free MFCs, direct electron transfer occurs without the need for mediators. Both types have their own advantages and limitations, and ongoing research is being conducted to improve their efficiency and feasibility.</p></p>
