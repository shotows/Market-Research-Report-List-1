<p><p>Blood apheresis collection systems are used to collect specific blood components for therapeutic purposes. The market types for these systems include centrifugation and membrane filtration. Centrifugation involves separating blood components by spinning them at high speeds, allowing heavier components to settle at the bottom. Membrane filtration, on the other hand, utilizes porous membranes to separate blood components based on their size. Both methods are crucial for collecting specific blood components needed for transfusion and therapeutic procedures.</p></p>
