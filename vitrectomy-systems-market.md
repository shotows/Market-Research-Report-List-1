<p><p>Vitrectomy systems are used for surgical procedures in various retinal conditions. Retinal detachment occurs when the retina separates from its underlying tissue. Macular puckers are the wrinkling of the macula, causing distorted vision. Diabetic retinopathy affects blood vessels in the retina due to diabetes. Macular holes are small breaks in the macula, leading to central vision loss. Vitreous hemorrhage involves bleeding into the vitreous gel, obstructing vision. Vitreous floaters are small specks or spots that drift in the vitreous, disturbing vision. Other conditions may include infections, tumors, or complications from eye surgery.</p></p>
